During this time, I also signed up to enroll in BOCES Seaway Technical Center in Norwood. One of my biggest accomplishments during my academic career was the creation of the SUNY Canton art diversity display in the campus public library. This display includes seven photo frames that capture key points and visually appealing projects across campus. While the art itself is important, the most valuable experience I’ve gained is how to effectively manage time and how to overcome all setbacks in my way.

Our first major setback was availability of team members and our clients. I am not on campus often as I commute, and one of my team members also commutes. This left us one member who could be consistently on campus. Not only that, contacting all of our clients through email left a bit of communication lag, which made it almost impossible to meet all clients. Nonetheless, I managed to power through and single handedly maintained contact with all parties involved.

Another setback was dealing with my teammates. I am the team leader of the diversity group, and I often struggled with my teammates in the beginning. They wouldn’t communicate effectively, and they did not have any incentive to do critical work. I had to learn to step up and take the reins, and directly tell them what their responsibilities were. I also learned to adapt to criticism and take advice into consideration.


Currently, I am now a Junior in SUNY Canton, and I am still managing to learn more and more about the ins and outs of coding. Currently, I am working on getting more skilled at the Java and XML languages, and I hope to take more coding classes in the future.
